About Edufocal

EduFocal is a social learning platform for Primary Exit Profile (PEP) students in Jamaica and Caribbean Secondary Education Certificate (CSEC) students in the Caribbean. The service uses gamification to present its test material to students on the service.

Students get to create profiles and specify the grade they are, so the subjects they will be taught will be specifically for that grade. They also work on assignments and tests, afterwards they get their reports.

Problem Statement

Students in primary schools need to be well equipped for their Primary Exit Profile (PEP) Exams. Most of the children will be between the ages of 8 and 12 years, so they need a platform that will be interesting to learn with and understand. Edufocal’s student dashboard will bridge the gap between these students and their success at their PEP exams.

How Might We?

Create a kid-friendly learning platform for PEP students.

Research and Discovery

The entire team had a scoping call, then research was carried out and here are some of the key findings:

Students need to be notified when they have an upcoming class
They should be able to keep track of their activities on the platform
Students need to be able to catch up on classes they missed
Students report cards need to be available for download
Our users are between ages 8 and 12 but parents will be the determinants if their children will be enrolled >

Solution

The solution is to create a student dashboard/learning platform that will be intuitive enough for the students to learn. A kid-friendly web application where students will always be willing to come and learn.

Business Impact

Students are required to pay a monthly fee to get access to classes, assignments and activities on the student dashboard. Therefore, this can serve as a revenue model for the business.

Student Dashboard

The student dashboard is designed in a way that only the most important things are on the dashboard: what will be happening today, the journal which records everything the student has done and classrooms which the students will be added to after signing up. These are the variations of how the dashboard will look when there are different activities coming up for the day.

Live Classes

This captures live classes (upcoming and past live classes).

Assignments and Journaln

Assignments section captures outstanding, submitted and assignments in progress.
Journal records the students activities on the platform daily, weekly and monthly.

Conclusion

The student dashboard was focused on majorly the most important activities the students need to carry out like classes they need to attend today, their outstanding assignments, if there is a report card available for download.

The interface was also designed to be kid-friendly because of our target audience.
